{% splith %}
    {% en %}What does Sitelen Pona look like?{% enden %}
    {% sp %}sitelen+pona li seme lukin{% endsp %}
    {% sl %}sitelen pona li seme lukin?{% endsl %}

{% endsplith %}

{% split %}
{% en %}
In Sitelen Pona, each [word](/method/words.html) can be represented by one simple [pictogram or ‘glyph'](/method/glyphs.html) that often looks like the thing it describes. Here are the symbols for *tomo* (house), *pipi* (bug), and *sike* (circle).
{% enden %}

{% sp %}
sitelen+pona la [nimi wan](/method/words.html) li sitelen nimi wan  
tenpo mute la sina sona ala e nasin pi(sitelen+pona) la   
zz sina awen ken sona e toki  tan lukin  tan [selo sitelen](/method/glyphs.html)  
zz sitelen nimi li sitelen e ijo nimi  
tomo en pipi en sike li sitelen e tomo e pipi e sike  
{% endsp %}

{% sl %}
sitelen pona la [nimi wan](/method/words.html) li sitelen wan. tenpo mute la sina sona ala e nasin pi sitelen pona la, sina awen ken sona e nimi sitelen tan lukin taso, tan [selo sitelen](/method.html). selo la sitelen li sitelen e ijo nimi. sitelen pona la 'tomo' en 'pipi' en 'sike' li sitelen e tomo e pipi e sike.  
{% endsl %}

{% endsplit %}

{% split %}
{% en %}
Sitelen Pona is usually written from left to right, from top to bottom. [Glyphs can be combined](/common.html#tier1punctuation). To say a bug’s house, *tomo pipi*, we might place the *pipi* glyph into the empty space in the tomo glyph. To talk about a circular bug or *pipi sike*, like ladybug, we might put the *sike* glyph above the *pipi* glyph.
{% enden %}

{% sp %}
nasin tawa pi(sitelen+pona) li sama sitelen [la:sina:] lon tenpo mute  
lukin o open lon sewi o pini lon anpa  
sitelen tu li [ken kama sama sitelen wan](common.html#tier1punctuation)   
tomo pipi la sitelen pipi li ken lon insa pi(sitelen tomo)  
zz o lukin pona e  tomo+pipi  
pipi sike la sitelen sike li ken lon sewi pi(sitelen pipi)  
zz o lukin pona e pipi-sike
{% endsp %}
{% sl %}
nasin tawa pi sitelen pona li sama sitelen Lasina lon tenpo mute. lukin o open lon sewi o pini lon anpa. sitelen tu li [ken kama sama sitelen wan](common.html#tier1punctuation). 'tomo pipi' la sitelen pipi li ken lon insa pi sitelen tomo. lon sitelen anpa la, o lukin pona e tomo pipi.  'pipi sike' la sitelen sike li ken lon sewi pi sitelen pipi. lon sitelen anpa la, o lukin pona e pipi sike. 
{% endsl %}

{% splith %}
    {% en %}Why use Sitelen Pona?{% enden %}
    {% sp %}sitelen+pona li pona tan seme{% endsp %}
    {% sl %}sitelen pona li pona tan seme?{% endsl %}

{% endsplith %}

{% split %}
{% en %}
The [majority](https://tokiponacensus.github.io/results2024/#writing-systems) of Toki Pona speakers can read and write Sitelen Pona, and many love to do so. Because Toki Pona has less than 150 words, many people find that writing it with pictographs is natural, efficient, and even fun. 

Because it’s such a visual script, some people find a text written with Sitelen Pona more intuitive to understand than the same text in the Latin alphabet.
{% enden %}

{% sp %}

sitelen+pona li pona mute tawa jan mute kulupu  
nimi pi(toki+pona )li mute lili la sitelen pi(toki+pona) li ken mute lili kin  
kepeken palisa sitelen la luka li ken sitelen+pona kepeken tenpo lili   
tan ni taso ala la sitelen+pona li musi pilin li pona pilin tawa jan mute  

sitelen pona li ken pona sona tawa lukin la jan pi mute lili li toki e ni  
te mi lukin e toki kepeken sitelen+pona la ken lukin mi li pona  
zz mi kepeken sitelen [la:sina:] la ken lukin mi li pona sama ala to
{% endsp %}
