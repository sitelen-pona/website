Hi everyone, we have several exciting announcements!

# Introducing [sitelenpona.net](https://sitelenpona.net)
sitelenpona.net is the new home of the **Sitelen Pona Publishers and Typographers Association**. Be sure to update your bookmarks and links! The new site is completely bilingual, with the Toki Pona available in Sitelen Pona and Latin text. 
If you’re not familiar, this is the organization we formed to develop standards for Sitelen Pona, with our long-term goal being the inclusion of Sitelen Pona in Unicode. This work has gone on for over two years now, and in that time, we’ve touched on almost every aspect of Sitelen Pona. Alongside the new Association homepage, we’d like to give you an update on what we’ve been working on.

# A proper hello to [Common Sitelen Pona](<https://sitelenpona.net/common>)
You may remember back in January when Common Sitelen Pona was first revealed. We received a lot of valuable feedback on that announcement, and we’ve gone to great lengths to address everything we missed before. You can read over the [**history of our efforts**](<https://sitelenpona.net/history>), see the details of [**every decision we’ve made**](<https://sitelenpona.net/method>), and get the answers to **all** your questions about the hidden complexities of Sitelen Pona.

# Introducing the [ASCII Input Standard](<https://sitelenpona.net/ascii>)
Also called “the ligature standard.” This is a standard way for fonts to write Sitelen Pona with ligatures, so that you get consistent results across different fonts when trying to combine glyphs, write cartouches, select glyph variants, and more. Previously, text written for one font would need to be manually adapted for another. With this standard, you’ll be able to get the same Sitelen Pona from any font you choose.

And here’s the best part: Thanks to [waso Keli](/members.html#waso-keli) and [her Sitelen Pona Font Maker](<https://wasokeli.github.io/sp-font-maker/>), the ASCII Input Standard is **already implemented in [over 130 fonts](<https://linku.la/fonts>)**, so you can start using it right away!
## Who is the standard for?
Anyone and everyone! Just as with Common Sitelen Pona, our mission is to serve all users of Sitelen Pona and make standards that reflect stable, long-term community usage.
## How do I use the standard?
For most people, all you need to do is start typing Sitelen Pona however you like. You can [download your preferred font](<https://linku.la/fonts>) to use for any purpose, or if on Discord, you can use ilo Linku’s /sp command to write in any font Linku offers.
If you create Sitelen Pona fonts and would like to implement the standard in an old or new one, [follow along with the documentation](<https://sitelenpona.net/ascii>). All your questions should be answered there; if they’re not, [talk to us](<https://sitelenpona.net/contact>).

# What’s next?
As noted at the start, our primary goal is the inclusion of Sitelen Pona in Unicode. We’ll be focusing our efforts here in the coming months, and part of that effort will be finalizing Common Sitelen Pona. (For what it’s worth, it’s ready for almost everyone as-is). We’re excited for what comes next, and we’ll share those updates when the time comes.

Until then, we’d like to cap this off with thanks. Whether you have made art with Sitelen Pona and pushed the boundaries of what it can do, or supported our work through surveys and collecting Sitelen Pona media, or simply participated in the community, thank you so much for your passion. Our work is built on everything the community has made and shared, and we will never take that for granted.

If you have any questions, [feel free to contact us](<https://sitelenpona.net/contact>), or email `toki@sitelenpona.net`.
And if you have any public Sitelen Pona works, whether fonts, articles, lessons, or anything else, consider [applying to join the Association](<https://docs.google.com/forms/d/e/1FAIpQLScDf9cWNQqxCMI_rELpvw49C7XCAuGNzMMuDAXFlxZ6E270Kg/viewform>). Thank you!
